Research

From the Energy & Emissions Research Group (EnERG), IIT Madras

The Energy & Emissions Research Group (EnERG) works across the industrial energy value chain — thermodynamic machines, sensors, storage, and AI — with a single aim: deep decarbonisation of industrial energy use.

Themes

Industrial decarbonisation
High-temperature and steam-generation heat pumps for electrifying process heat — including India's first 120 °C industrial heat pump deployment.
Steam expanders and pressure recovery
The patented Wankel expander converts wasted steam pressure-reduction into power — an estimated 20 GW opportunity in India.
ORC waste-heat recovery
Organic Rankine cycle systems, including a patented two-stage regenerative configuration.
MSME energy efficiency
At-scale energy auditing methodology through KISEM — 255 firms across 30 sectors.
Energy storage
Vanadium redox flow, zinc-air, and compressed-air storage (patented recuperated CAES).
Optical and spectroscopic sensing
Steam-quality TDLAS, moisture-independent fly-ash stack sensors, LIBS diagnostics.
Fuel cells and CCHP
Solid oxide fuel cells and combined cooling-heat-power for data centres.
AUSC power-plant materials
High-temperature steam oxidation of advanced ultra-supercritical materials.
AI for Net-Zero
Physics-informed machine learning for building energy.
