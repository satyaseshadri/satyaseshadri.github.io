Satyanarayanan Seshadri is an Indian engineer and academic, Professor (since December 2025) and Shell Chair Professor (since 2024) in the Department of Applied Mechanics and Biomedical Engineering at the Indian Institute of Technology Madras. He works on decarbonising industrial energy use in India — pioneering high-temperature industrial heat pumps and steam expanders, and building a national industry–academia–government energy-transition ecosystem.[1]

His research portfolio spans 211 sponsored projects worth ₹393 crore (₹276 crore as principal investigator), 40 Q1 journal publications,[2] and 12 patent filings across 9 inventions. He co-founded four deep-tech startups and describes his translational model as “research to relevance to revenue”.

Education

Seshadri received a B.E. in Mechanical Engineering from the University of Madras in 2002, graduating as Gold Medalist. He earned an M.S. (2004) and Ph.D. (2007) in Mechanical Engineering from Texas A&M University, and was a Postdoctoral Associate at Rutgers University (2007–2008).

Career

Before academia, Seshadri spent nearly a decade in industrial R&D — as Lead Engineer at GE Global Research Center, Bangalore (2008–2013) and as R&D Head / Head of Exploratory R&D at Forbes Marshall (2013–2015). He joined IIT Madras in 2015 as Assistant Professor, became Associate Professor in 2021, and was promoted to Professor in December 2025. In 2024 he was appointed the inaugural Shell Chair Professor.[3]

Research

Main article: Research

Seshadri's group works on industrial decarbonisation: high-temperature and steam-generation heat pumps, steam expanders and pressure recovery (the patented Wankel expander), organic Rankine cycle waste-heat recovery, MSME energy efficiency, energy storage (vanadium redox flow, zinc-air, compressed-air), optical and spectroscopic sensing, solid oxide fuel cells for data centres, advanced ultra-supercritical (AUSC) power-plant materials, and physics-informed machine learning for building energy.

He deployed the first 120 °C industrial heat pump in India, with emission intensity below 45% of baseline, and his very-low-ambient heat pumps operate in Leh, Ladakh and Sikkim down to −35 °C.

Centres and leadership

Main article: Leadership & centres

Seshadri founded and heads The Energy Consortium (2021–), India's first industry–academia–government energy consortium (52 faculty, 12 industry members), which represented India at COP28.[4] He founded KISEM, the Kotak IIT Madras Save Energy Mission (2022–), adjudged India's best energy audit firm at the SEEM 2024 awards. He is Head of the School of Innovation and Entrepreneurship (2025–), leads the Shell IITM Centre for Energy Research (SICER, 2024–), chairs the Australia India Centre for Energy (AICE), directs the Centre of Excellence in Energy and Telecom (CEET), and co-launched the IIT Madras–Coal India Centre for Sustainable Energy (October 2025).[5]

Ventures

Main article: Ventures

Seshadri has co-founded four deep-tech startups: Wankel Energy Systems (2022, steam-to-power; MIT Clean Energy Prize 2024 finalist — top 8 of 163, the only Indian startup), TrigenDC (2023, industrial heating electrification; FLCTD/UNIDO award winner 2022), EnergyEta (2024, building-energy OS), and AranTree Consulting (2024, ESG and decarbonisation advisory), alongside the IndusDC venture studio.[6] As Faculty-in-charge of the Nirmaan pre-incubator (2017–2022) he helped seed 20+ startups now collectively valued at about ₹1,000 crore.

Selected works

Main articles: Publications and Patents

Seshadri has published 40 Q1 journal papers (2004–2026), 30 conference papers, and 4 books/chapters, in venues including Energy Conversion and Management, Energy, Journal of Energy Storage, and ACS Sensors. As of April 2026 his work has 714 citations (h-index 13).[2] Key patents include the Wankel expander admission-volume control (US 11,448,073; India 507179).
